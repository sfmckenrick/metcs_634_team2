Our Friends

Bands

Skating Polly
There's a difficult to describe, yet timeless quality to certain songs that transcends genre or era. It's something that you can't fake or contrive and it's what lies at the core of Skating Polly's music.

PARTYBABY
A Los Angeles-based modern rock/garage punk quartet fronted by scene veterans Jamie Reed (30 Seconds to Mars) and Noah Gersh (Portugal. The Man), PARTYBABY's decibel-pushing brand of propulsive and hook-driven hard rock draws inspiration from artists like Nirvana, Cheap Trick, the Stooges, Weezer, Smashing Pumpkins, and the Cars. In late 2015 PARTYBABY issued a pair of high-octane singles, "Your Old Man" and "Everything's Alright," in anticipation of their first full-length outing, The Golden Age of Bullshit, which dropped in September 2016 via Warner Bros.

Beach Slang
Feelings of youth and vulnerability lie at the core of Beach Slang's music, which is part punk, part pop and all catharsis. It references the ghosts of The Replacements but keeps one foot firmly rooted in the present. It's fun and it's serious. It's sad but it isn't. It's Beach Slang.

Venues

Iron Horse Music Hall
The Iron Horse Music Hall is a music venue located in Northampton, Massachusetts, west of Boston and north of Springfield, Massachusetts. Its motto is Music Alone Shall Live. The music has showcased a wide variety of musical genres and performing artists in past years, including artists such as Richie Havens, Leo Kottke, Janis Ian, Livingston Taylor, Duke Robillard, Ellis Paul and Vance Gilbert.

Calvin Theatre
Spacious, historical theater serving as a concert venue for a range of acclaimed music artists.
